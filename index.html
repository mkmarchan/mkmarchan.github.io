AIRWAVE

AIRWAVE was a collaborative project between Mike Notter and I for the Sound and Media Computer Science and Engineering Capstone (CSE481i). The aim was to create a better way to control granular synthesis in real-time. Current granular synthesis controllers are difficult to use in a live setting as there are too many parameters to manipulate with just knobs and sliders. By tracking the orientation and position of a user's hands with a Leap Motion sensor, we were able to unlock simultaneous control of 6 synthesis parameters (volume, panning, grain trigger frequency, grain duration, playback speed, and grain index).

LBHRTAM

Low-Budget Head Rotation Tracking and Ambisonic Mapping (LBHRTAM) is my Sound in Space (DXARTS 567) final project where I create a low-budget solution to monitoring ambisonic audio. This solution involves strapping an Android smartphone to your head and having it send orientation information to a computer running a SuperCollider script that rotates a soundfield accordingly.
